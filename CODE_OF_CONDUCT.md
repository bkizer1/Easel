# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We pledge that everyone participating in the Easel project and its communities will be treated with respect and dignity, and that the community will remain free from harassment, discrimination, and other negative behavior.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy towards other community members

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment of any kind (verbal, physical, sexual, etc.)
- Discriminatory language or behavior based on race, ethnicity, gender, sexual orientation, disability, age, religion, or any other protected characteristic
- Deliberate intimidation or threats
- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, or personal attacks
- Publishing private information (doxxing) without consent
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Any conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Enforcement

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it to:

- **Email:** blake.kizer@gmail.com
- **GitHub:** Open a private security advisory at [GitHub Security](https://github.com/bkizer1/Easel/security/advisories)

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. All community leaders are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including:

- GitHub issues, pull requests, and discussions
- Project repositories and code
- Community forums and chat channels
- Social media posts and comments related to Easel
- Any other spaces where community members interact on behalf of the Easel project

## Consequences

Violations of the Code of Conduct may result in:

- A warning from community leaders
- Temporary or permanent banning from project participation
- Removal of contributions deemed inappropriate

The severity of consequences will be determined on a case-by-case basis, considering the nature and context of the violation.

## Appeal Process

If you believe enforcement action was taken in error, you may appeal by emailing blake.kizer@gmail.com with a clear explanation. Appeals will be reviewed fairly and promptly.
